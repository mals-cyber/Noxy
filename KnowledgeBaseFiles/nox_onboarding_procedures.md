## 1. PRE-ONBOARDING (Before First Day)

### 1.1 Document Preparation
New employees must prepare the following documents before their first day:

**Government Requirements:**
- Valid Government-Issued IDs (at least 2)
  - Driver's License
  - Passport
  - Postal ID
  - PhilSys ID
  - Voter's ID

- Social Security System (SSS)
  - SSS Number
  - SSS E-1 Form (Member's Data Record)
  - If no SSS yet, company will assist in registration

- PhilHealth
  - PhilHealth Number
  - PhilHealth Member Data Record (MDR)
  - If no PhilHealth yet, company will assist in registration

- Pag-IBIG (Home Development Mutual Fund)
  - Pag-IBIG MID Number
  - Pag-IBIG Member's Data Form
  - If no Pag-IBIG yet, company will assist in registration

- Tax Identification Number (TIN)
  - TIN Card or TIN Number
  - BIR Form 1902 (for first-time jobseeker) or BIR Form 2316 (from previous employer)

**Clearance Documents:**
- NBI Clearance (not older than 6 months)
  OR
- Police Clearance (not older than 6 months)

**Educational Documents:**
- Diploma (Certified True Copy)
- Transcript of Records (Certified True Copy)
- Certificate of Grades (if recently graduated)
- Any relevant certifications or training certificates

**Employment History:**
- Certificate of Employment from previous employers (if applicable)
- BIR Form 2316 from previous employer (for tax purposes)

**Personal Documents:**
- Birth Certificate (PSA-issued Certified True Copy)
- Marriage Certificate (if applicable, PSA-issued)
- 2x2 ID Photos (at least 4 pieces, white background)

**Bank Information:**
- Bank account details for salary crediting
- Bank name, branch, account number, account name

### 1.2 Health Requirements
- Pre-employment Medical Examination results
- Chest X-ray (if required)
- Drug Test results
- Any health declarations or medical history forms

### 1.3 Pre-Onboarding Communication
- Check email for onboarding schedule and instructions
- Confirm attendance and receipt of onboarding materials
- Note the reporting time, date, and location
- Prepare questions about the role or company

## 2. FIRST DAY ONBOARDING

### 2.1 Welcome and Introduction (Morning Session)
**8:00 AM - 9:00 AM: Registration and Welcome**
- Report to HR Department at designated office (Cebu or Manila)
- Sign attendance and receive visitor/temporary badge
- Complete employee registration forms
- Submit all required documents
- Review and sign employment contract
- Receive employee handbook

**9:00 AM - 10:00 AM: HR Orientation**
- Company overview and history presentation
- Vision, mission, and core values
- Organizational structure
- Company policies and code of conduct
- Employee benefits briefing (HMO, leaves, holidays)
- Work schedule and attendance policies
- Introduction to NOX onboarding system

**10:00 AM - 10:15 AM: Break**

**10:15 AM - 11:30 AM: Administrative Setup**
- ID photo capture
- Issuance of employee ID card
- Email account setup
- Introduction to company communication tools
- Building access and security briefing
- Tour of office facilities
- Introduction to floor wardens and emergency procedures

**11:30 AM - 12:00 PM: Benefits Enrollment**
- Complete HMO enrollment forms
- Designate HMO beneficiaries
- Set up payroll bank account (if not yet done)
- Complete government benefit forms (SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG)
- Tax withholding setup

### 2.2 Afternoon Session
**1:00 PM - 2:30 PM: IT Setup and Orientation**
- Issuance of computer and equipment
- Setup of workstation
- Email and system access provisioning
- Introduction to development tools and platforms
- Network access and VPN setup (if applicable)
- Cybersecurity and data privacy briefing
- Password policies and information security guidelines

**2:30 PM - 3:30 PM: Department Introduction**
- Meet department head and team members
- Overview of department structure and functions
- Introduction to current projects
- Role and responsibilities briefing
- Performance expectations and KPIs
- Team collaboration tools and practices

**3:30 PM - 3:45 PM: Break**

**3:45 PM - 4:30 PM: Introduction to NOX System**
- NOX (N-PAX Onboarding Expert) system walkthrough
- Meet Noxy, your AI assistant
- How to upload and track documents
- Using the chatbot for onboarding questions
- Accessing onboarding materials
- How to request HR consultation
- Review onboarding progress tracker

**4:30 PM - 5:00 PM: First Day Wrap-up**
- Q&A session
- Review first week schedule
- Assign onboarding buddy/mentor
- Provide reading materials for overnight review
- Set expectations for Week 1

## 3. FIRST WEEK ONBOARDING

### Week 1 Focus: Integration and Learning

**Monday (Day 1):**
- Complete all Day 1 activities as outlined above
- Evening: Review employee handbook and company policies

**Tuesday (Day 2):**
- Morning: Product orientation (HRC and N-XPERT overview)
- Access to learning management system/training materials
- Begin technical training based on role
- Shadow team members
- Afternoon: Start reviewing codebase or project documentation

**Wednesday (Day 3):**
- Continue technical training
- Attend team meetings as observer
- Setup development environment completely
- Complete mandatory compliance training modules
- Practice using company tools and systems

**Thursday (Day 4):**
- Begin hands-on work with guidance
- First assignment or training project
- Code review or quality assurance observation
- Attend knowledge sharing session or tech talk
- Meet with other departments (cross-functional introduction)

**Friday (Day 5):**
- Continue training assignments
- First week check-in with supervisor
- Complete any pending documentation
- End-of-week reflection and Q&A
- Social integration (team lunch or coffee, if applicable)

### Weekly Milestones to Achieve:
✓ All documents submitted and approved in NOX system
✓ Complete understanding of company policies
✓ Email and systems access fully functional
✓ Basic understanding of role and team structure
✓ Completed mandatory training modules
✓ Development environment setup complete
✓ Met all immediate team members
✓ Familiar with office layout and facilities

## 4. FIRST MONTH ONBOARDING

### Week 2-4 Focus: Skill Building and Contribution

**Week 2:**
- Deeper technical training specific to assigned projects
- Begin contributing to non-critical tasks
- Participate actively in team meetings
- Continue using NOX chatbot for questions
- Schedule one-on-one meetings with key team members
- Complete any pending training modules

**Week 3:**
- Take on more responsibility in projects
- Receive first code review or work review
- Attend client meetings or project discussions (as appropriate)
- Start understanding client requirements and business context
- Build relationships with cross-functional teams

**Week 4:**
- End of probationary month check-in
- Performance feedback session with supervisor
- Self-assessment of first month
- Clarify any outstanding questions
- Confirm understanding of performance expectations
- Review benefits activation (HMO should be active by now)
- Ensure all onboarding requirements are complete in NOX

### First Month Milestones:
✓ All government benefits enrolled and processed
✓ HMO active and dependent enrollment complete
✓ First project contribution made
✓ Understanding of development processes
✓ Comfortable with tools and technologies used
✓ Building productive working relationships
✓ Clear understanding of performance metrics
✓ All onboarding training completed

## 9. COMMON ONBOARDING QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

**Q: When will I receive my employee ID?**
A: Your employee ID will be issued on your first day after photo capture, typically within the first few hours. You'll receive a temporary badge until your permanent ID is ready if needed.

**Q: How long does it take for my email account to be activated?**
A: Email accounts are typically activated on your first day. The IT team will set this up during your morning orientation session.

**Q: When does my HMO coverage start?**
A: HMO coverage typically begins on your first day of employment. You'll receive your HMO card within 2-4 weeks, but coverage is effective immediately.

**Q: Can I add dependents to my HMO?**
A: Yes, you can add eligible dependents. Complete the dependent enrollment form provided by HR during onboarding.

**Q: When will I receive my first salary?**
A: Your first salary will be processed according to the company's payroll schedule. HR will provide specific dates during orientation. Note that your first pay may be prorated.

**Q: What happens if I don't have all required documents on Day 1?**
A: You must have at least the mandatory documents (valid IDs, NBI clearance, educational credentials) to start. Other documents can be submitted within a grace period, which will be specified by HR.

**Q: Is the probationary period standard?**
A: The typical probationary period at N-PAX is 5-6 months, as per Philippine Labor Law. This period allows both you and the company to assess fit.

**Q: Can I work from home during onboarding?**
A: First week typically requires office presence for setup and orientation. Work-from-home arrangements, if applicable to your role, will be discussed with your supervisor.

**Q: Who is my onboarding buddy?**
A: An onboarding buddy or mentor will be assigned on your first day. This person will help you navigate your first weeks and answer day-to-day questions.

**Q: How do I use the NOX system?**
A: NOX training is provided on your first day. You'll receive login credentials and a walkthrough of all features. Noxy, the AI chatbot, is available 24/7 to answer questions.

**Q: What if I have a question outside of office hours?**
A: Use the NOX system and chat with Noxy anytime. For urgent matters that require human intervention, contact your supervisor or HR during business hours.

**Q: Are there training opportunities beyond onboarding?**
A: Yes, N-PAX values continuous learning. Technical training, workshops, and professional development opportunities are available throughout your employment.

**Q: What is expected of me during the probationary period?**
A: During probation, you're expected to demonstrate: technical competence, good communication, teamwork, adherence to policies, willingness to learn, and consistent performance. Your supervisor will provide regular feedback.
